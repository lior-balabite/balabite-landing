# NRA Booth — Demo Script

**For:** Lior, solo, Booth 8332 · NRA Show 2026 · May 16–19
**Runs on:** `/demo` (laptop) — the guided walk of the 3 Pulse screens
**Length:** 2–3 minutes start to finish. The elevator pitch is the 10-second version.

> Source of truth: this is translated from `ShortDeck.tsx` slides 1–6 and the brief's
> 4 takeaways — no new narrative. Customer-facing language is **"AI Cofounder."**
> Never say a price. Never say the word "chef." Talk to the **person**, not the business.

---

## The 10-second elevator pitch + handshake line

Say this when someone slows down near the booth — before you ever open the laptop.

> "BalaBite is the **AI Cofounder** for an independent restaurant. You run the place —
> it does the rest. And I'm not a software guy who studied restaurants — **I live in
> one. I'm the engineer and the operator.** Want to see it?"

That last line is the whole credibility play. Land it, then pause. If they say yes,
open the laptop to `/demo`. If they've only got a second, that pitch stands on its own.

---

## The walk — 5 steps, ~2 minutes

Each heading below is one screen of `/demo`. The **[bracketed]** notes are stage
directions; the quoted text is what you actually say. Click **"Show me →" / "Next →"**
to advance; the screen animates as you talk, so you don't have to rush it.

### 1 — Open (~15 sec)
*[Laptop open to `/demo`. The opening line is already on screen.]*

> "Here's the thing nobody warns you about when you open a restaurant. You end up
> running a dozen tools that don't talk to each other — POS, payroll, reviews,
> delivery, marketing — and **you're the only thing connecting them.** You're the
> integration layer. Alone. And the math on a restaurant won't pay for the team that
> would take that off you. So we built one. Let me show you what it actually does."

*[Click "Show me →".]*

### 2 — It sees *(Screen 1 · the Pulse)* (~30 sec)
*[The morning-briefing screen. Highlights land on the synthesis, then the decision.]*

> "This is the Pulse — the morning briefing. It read the whole restaurant overnight:
> menu, revenue, customers, operations, all of it. And it's **not a dashboard you dig
> through** — it just tells you, in plain words, how the place is doing. Notice it's
> talking to the owner *by name.*
>
> And here's the part — out of everything it found, there's **one decision waiting.**
> A reprice. One thing it needs you for. It's not handing you a to-do list. It's
> handing you the day."

**Transition:** *"So that's what it sees. Here's what it did about it."* → Click Next.

### 3 — It acts *(Screen 2 · running in the background)* (~30 sec)
*[The background-workflows screen. Highlight lands on "Handled automatically."]*

> "While you were running service last night, *this* was running too. Six things
> going at once, five more queued. And look at this section — **'handled
> automatically.'** It rewrote menu descriptions. It adjusted prep quantities for the
> weather forecast. It sent a re-engagement email to eight regulars who'd gone quiet.
>
> Nobody told it to. It didn't ask. It just handled it. **Sometimes it asks you — but
> mostly, it acts.**"

**Transition:** *"Now — the part that tells you whether this is a vendor or a partner."* → Click Next.

### 4 — It owns it *(Screen 3 · this week's record)* (~30 sec)
*[The weekly-record screen. Highlight lands on "Didn't land."]*

> "This is the week, graded. **What worked** — retention's up, food cost is down on
> the items it flagged. **What's still in progress.** And then this: **'didn't
> land.'** It tried a happy-hour pricing experiment. It pulled revenue per order
> down — so it got reverted. And it *tells you that.*
>
> A vendor never tells you their thing failed. **A cofounder does.** That section
> exists on purpose."

**Transition:** *"That's it — sees, acts, owns it. Three screens."* → Click Next.

### 5 — Close (~15 sec)
*[The closing screen with the booking CTA.]*

> "And this is **one real restaurant, live since February** — real POS data, three
> years of orders, not a demo environment. What you just saw is a taste. The real
> thing is *your* numbers. Give me twenty minutes after the show and I'll run your
> restaurant through it — you'll see exactly what it would catch."
>
> "Can I grab a time?"

*[Turn the laptop toward them. Click "Book 20 minutes →" — it opens the booking
directly. Let them pick the slot themselves.]*

## If they only have 30 seconds

Skip the walk. Elevator pitch, then:

> "Three screens. It **sees** your whole restaurant in one morning briefing. It
> **acts** — handles the busywork overnight without being asked. And it **owns it** —
> it even tells you what it tried that *didn't* work. One restaurant, live since
> February. Want me to run yours? Twenty minutes."

Then go straight to the booking.

---

## The 4 takeaways — what every version has to land

Whatever you cut for time, the prospect should leave with these:

1. **The role outgrew the human.** They're the only integration layer — alone.
2. **BalaBite runs the rest.** The team the math never let them hire.
3. **It acts — mostly it acts — and it owns the outcome**, including what failed.
4. **Yours alone.** Their POS's AI is every competitor's AI. BalaBite lives in *their*
   restaurant, not the category's.

## Delivery notes

- **Pace.** The screen animates while you talk — let the highlights land before you
  point at them. Don't out-run the motion.
- **Don't read the screen.** They can see it. Your job is the *meaning* of what's on it.
- **The honesty beat (Screen 3) is the close, really.** "Didn't land" is what
  separates you from every other booth on that floor. Slow down there.
- **Never say the price.** If they ask, "It's partner economics, not software pricing
  — I'll walk you through it in the twenty minutes." Then book the time.
- **One voice.** BalaBite is a singular presence — "it," never "they."
- **Fallback:** if booth wifi dies, `/demo` still runs (the screens are local images).
  Worst case, the TV loop's `.mp4` is on your laptop and phone — see
  `docs/nra/README.md`.
