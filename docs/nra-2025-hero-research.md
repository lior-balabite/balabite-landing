# NRA 2025 Report: Extracted Intelligence for Hero Visual

Source: National Restaurant Association — State of the Restaurant Industry 2025

---

## THE PROFITABILITY CRISIS

| Stat | Source |
|---|---|
| **39% of operators say their restaurant was NOT profitable in 2024** | p.11 |
| **53% still carrying pandemic debt** | p.11 |
| **3-5% pre-tax profit margins** — the only margin they have to work with | p.34 |
| Menu prices up **>28% since Feb 2020** just to maintain that 5% margin | p.34 |
| Food and labor costs both up **>30% since 2019** | p.11, p.65 |
| **61% of operators reported customer traffic DECLINED** between 2023-2024 | p.11 |
| **18% expect sales to DROP** in 2025 | p.12 |

## THE COST PRESSURE TOWER (what's crushing them)

| Cost Category | All Restaurants |
|---|---|
| **Labor costs** | **96%** |
| **Food costs** | **95%** |
| **Insurance costs** | **88%** |
| **Energy/utility costs** | **83%** |

96% say labor is a problem. 95% say food costs. Almost every single operator.

## THE EMOTIONAL WEIGHT

The NRA report frames this as "opportunities for growth in an unpredictable operating environment" — but between the lines:

- The operator is simultaneously: chef, HR manager, accountant, marketer, therapist, janitor, and customer service rep
- They're raising prices just to stay flat, cutting staff, cutting hours, cutting days
- They're carrying pandemic debt while food costs soar
- **The balance metaphor is LITERAL**: the report title section is "A Balance of Optimism & Hard Work" — they're balancing on a knife's edge
