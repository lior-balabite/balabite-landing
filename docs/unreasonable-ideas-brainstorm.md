# Unreasonable Ideas Brainstorm — 2026-03-23
## Purple Cow concepts for Balabite

Note: Many of these are broader marketing/product demo concepts, not just landing page ideas. Marked accordingly.

---

### LANDING PAGE CONCEPTS

#### 1. THE SHIFT (interactive experience)
The page IS a restaurant shift. Orders tick in, staff texts pop up, POS freezes, Yelp reviews appear. User can't keep up. Screen fills with chaos. Then everything freezes. "You do this every day. What if you didn't do it alone?"

#### 2. THE DARE (Fig-proven interactive collapse)
"Add one more thing to your plate." Red button. Each click adds an item to the chaos pile. Board tilts more. After ~10 clicks, everything COLLAPSES. Items scatter. Then rearrange into 9 neat stations with characters.

#### 3. THE SPLIT SCREEN (Apple before/after)
50/50 split. Left: "6:00 AM WITHOUT" (dark, chaotic, notifications cascading). Right: "6:00 AM WITH" (bright, calm, one notification, coffee being sipped). Both scroll together. Divider slides left — "with" side takes over.

#### 4. THE TICKER (Spotify Wrapped meets stock exchange)
Live data ticker across top: "47,000 restaurant owners are still at work right now... $2.3M in food wasted today..." Real NRA data, extrapolated. Below: "These aren't statistics. This is Tuesday."

#### 5. THE RECEIPT (thermal paper scrolling hero)
Entire hero is a scrolling thermal receipt. Monospace font, paper texture. Line items mix operational and emotional: "FIRES TODAY: 23 / HOURS WORKED: 14.5 / FAMILY DINNERS MISSED: 1 / TOTAL: 3.2% MARGIN / THANK YOU FOR YOUR SERVICE (no one else will)"

#### 6. THE CLOCK
Sticky counter: "Time you've spent on this page: 0:47." Then: "In that time, you could have had your entire morning briefing." Product is faster than the landing page explaining it.

### MARKETING / VIDEO / PRODUCT DEMO CONCEPTS

#### 7. THE PHONE (full phone screen experience)
Entire page is a realistic iPhone at 6am. Notifications cascade from overnight. 23 alerts. Then Balabite notification slides in: "Everything's handled." Tap to unlock into briefing. Format IS the pitch.

#### 8. THE VOICEMAIL (emotional audio hook)
Page loads with a play button. Black screen. Voicemail from spouse: "The kids asked where you were again... come home tonight." Then: "The restaurant will always need more of you. Unless it doesn't."
- Risk: could feel manipulative. Must be handled with genuine care.

#### 9. THE LAST ONE TO LEAVE (reverse-scroll cinema)
Page scrolls backward through an operator's day. 11:47 PM alone doing schedules → 6:00 AM arriving to empty parking lot. Then reverses to forward — same 6:00 AM but with Balabite notification. "What if today, you were the last to leave because you chose to be?"

#### 10. THE EMPTY CHAIR (Patagonia energy)
Full viewport photo: family dinner table, three people eating, one empty chair, plate getting cold. No text. Scroll to: "The restaurant always comes first. Until it doesn't have to."

#### 11. THE TEXTS (iMessage thread)
Above-the-fold is an iMessage thread — staff group chat, vendor messages, bank alerts. The unread "Mom: Haven't heard from you in weeks" is the gut punch. Then Balabite thread appears: clean, calm, handled.

#### 12. THE NAME (Superhuman personalization)
Input field: "What's your restaurant called?" User types. Page personalizes: "Good morning, [name]. Here's what Balabite would have caught last night." Simulated briefing with realistic data.

#### 13. THE SOUND (additive to any concept)
Opt-in audio. Restaurant ambient noise — tickets printing, pans, hood vent. Builds as you scroll through chaos. Goes SILENT at the turn. Then: a coffee cup being set down. Layerable on top of any concept.

---

### THE MASHUP (full experience concept)
Combine: Phone + Texts + Sound + Receipt. Full 45-second emotional experience recreating the 6am phone check, then healing it. The operator's most dreaded moment, rebuilt and resolved on screen.
